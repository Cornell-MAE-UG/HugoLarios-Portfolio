Who Am I

My team participates in a yearly competition held by the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA). Every year, AIAA creates a new set of mission parameters for teams to follow, meaning that my team must design a new plane for each competition. As the propulsion subteam lead, I determine the electronic components to be used in the plane, create integration pieces for these components, and testing plans to determine their calculated vs. real efficiencies.

Here's some of the work that
I've done on my project team:

  • Created a testing rig to measure static thrust force from any battery and motor combination.
  • Used Fusion 360 and Solidworks to CAD integration pieces for the interior of an RC plane
  • Programmed a flight controller to navigate itself without pilot control.
  • Machined metal motor mount pieces to implement on either the plane or testing rig.
  • CADed a new 3D printed nose cone to reduce form drag and reduce the weight in the front of the plane.

Skills

Over the past couple of years, I've been learning and practicing new skills that are relevant to the engineering workforce.

Programming

I can program in Python, Java, Arduino, and MATLAB. I used Arduino to program a HX711 load cell to translate strain into applied force, which I then used to measure static thrust force from our motor and propeller. Python and MATLAB are typically used in my classwork, where I often use them to calculate or predict behavior.

3D Design

I've been able to use Fusion 360 ever since my senior year of high school, having been taught to use them to design robots for my robotics club. I learned to use both Solidworks and Ansys Workspace on my project team, where I used them to design and simulate components under load and air streams.

Development

I'm machine shopped certified at my school, where I learned to use CNC machining and milling. I've used this certification to create a motor mount to use on our static thrust testing rig, as well as other steel or aluminum components. I also know how to crimp and solder wires, which I've used to integrate different electrical compoenents into the plane and make them compatible.
